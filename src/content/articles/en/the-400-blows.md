Antoine Doinel is no delinquent: he is a child no one listens to. Truffaut turns his own
adolescence into the founding charter of the New Wave, shot in the real streets of Paris
with a camera finally let out to breathe.

## Cinema as escape

School punishes, home suffocates, the street promises. Antoine steals, lies and flees
not out of malice but out of an instinct for air. When he slips into a cinema, stealing
a poster is almost an act of love.

> Sometimes I tell the truth and they don't believe me, so I prefer to lie.

## The run to the sea

The final shot follows him as he runs toward a sea he has never seen. And when he
reaches it, there is no liberation: the image freezes on his face as he turns to the
camera, unsure what to do with his freedom. The whole of cinema holds its breath with
him.
