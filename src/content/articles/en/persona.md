*Persona* opens with a projector flaring to life and closes with one going dark.
In between, Bergman dismantles cinema while building it, leaving us one of the most
dizzying meditations on identity ever filmed.

## The actress who falls silent

Elisabet, an actress, decides to stop speaking. Alma, the nurse who cares for her,
cannot stop. One woman's silence absorbs the other's words until the two faces begin
to merge, literally, in a shot the history of cinema has never forgotten.

> Perhaps I dream that someone exists who is me. No, that's too much. Not that far.

## Cinema that breaks

Midway through, the image burns, the celluloid tears. Bergman reminds us we are
watching light projected on a wall, a fragile illusion. That rupture isn't a trick:
it is the film's thesis made matter.

## Porous identities

Are we a stable self or a stack of masks — *personae* — we adopt before others?
Bergman offers no answer. He sets two faces before the mirror and lets the viewer
decide which one is the reflection.
