In the rain, among the ruins of the Rashomon gate, three men try to reconstruct what
happened in a forest: a dead samurai, his wife, a bandit. Each witness tells a different
version. None lies entirely; none is true.

## The camera enters the forest

Kurosawa films sunlight through the leaves as no one had before: the light itself seems
unreliable, flickering, complicit in the deception. The camera moves with a freedom that
astonished Western cinema.

> Men are unable to be honest with themselves.

## Relativism as form

Each version flatters its teller. It isn't that they lie: it's that each one needs to be
the hero of his own crime. Kurosawa turns that frailty into narrative structure.

## A thread of faith

When all seems lost, the woodcutter takes in an abandoned baby. A minimal gesture that
solves no mystery, but restores, under the clearing rain, a small reason to believe in
someone.
