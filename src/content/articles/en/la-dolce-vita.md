Marcello, a society journalist, drifts through a Rome of parties, celebrities and
emptiness. Fellini abandons classical narrative and builds the film as a succession of
nights, each more dazzling and more hollow than the last.

## The image that defines an era

The scene of Anita Ekberg in the Trevi Fountain was engraved into the century's memory.
Yet Fellini surrounds it with melancholy: splendour is always an instant about to go
dark.

> You must leave everything and start again, just once.

## The witness who doesn't act

Marcello could write, love, commit. Instead he watches. He is the chronicler of a world
dissolving and, without noticing, dissolves with it. From his trade, in passing, comes
the word *paparazzi*.

## The monster on the beach

At dawn, after another wasted night, the revellers find a sea creature beached on the
shore. A girl calls to Marcello from across the sand, but the noise of the sea swallows
her voice. Purity exists, but we can no longer hear it.
