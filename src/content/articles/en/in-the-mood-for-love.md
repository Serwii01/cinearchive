*In the Mood for Love* is a film about restraint. Two neighbours discover that their
spouses are cheating with each other and, instead of seeking comfort in one another,
build a love they never quite allow to exist.

## The gesture and the threshold

Wong Kar-wai stages the encounters in narrow corridors, stairwells, half-open doors.
Space compresses the characters until every brush of contact feels like an event.
The camera slows time when she goes down for noodles, and that trivial gesture turns
liturgical.

> He remembers those vanished years. As though looking through a dusty window pane,
> the past is something he could see but not touch.

## The dress as narration

Maggie Cheung's *cheongsam* aren't costume: they are chapters. Each change marks a
temporal ellipsis, a mood, an emotional border. The repetition of patterns turns
fashion into grammar.

## The music of desire

Shigeru Umebayashi's waltz returns again and again like a thought that won't leave.
*In the Mood for Love* understands that the most intense desire is the one left
unconsummated, and makes of that renunciation its purest form of beauty.
