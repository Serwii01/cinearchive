A Belarusian teenager joins the partisans during the Nazi occupation. What follows is not
a war film: it is a descent into hell, filmed from within a single gaze.

## The ageing of a face

Klimov fixes the camera on Flyora's face and turns it into the true landscape. Across the
film, the boy ages decades in hours: horror inscribes itself on his skin, in his hollowed
eyes, until he becomes unrecognisable.

> Don't shoot. I didn't want to kill a man.

## The sound of trauma

The constant ringing after a bombardment, the subjective camera, the faces staring
directly at the viewer: Klimov denies us all distance. We don't watch the war, we endure
it.

## The impossible shot

In the final sequence, Flyora fires at a portrait of Hitler and history reels backward
through archival footage... until it halts before a photograph of a baby. It is the one
limit the boy — and the film — refuse to cross.
