A village of farmers hires seven masterless warriors to defend it from bandits. From
that premise Kurosawa draws three and a half hours that never waste a minute, and the
narrative template for half a century of cinema to come.

## The grammar of action

Kurosawa films with telephoto lenses, multiple cameras and an editing sense of space
matched by no one. The final battle in rain and mud is not chaos: it is filmed chess,
each death placed with geometric precision.

> Again we lose. The winners are the farmers, not us.

## Heroes of a dying world

The real subject isn't victory but its melancholy. The samurai win yet do not belong:
their age is over. Kambei says so before his comrades' graves. Those who sow survive,
not those who wield the sword. Honour, here, is a beautiful way of being left outside
history.
