The protagonist of Bresson's most spiritual film is a donkey. Balthazar passes from
owner to owner, receiving blows and, sometimes, tenderness, while human cruelty unfolds
around him with the naturalness of a changing season.

## The model, not the actor

Bresson rejects acting: his "models" recite without emphasis, gestures pared to the
essential. The result is a stripped-down screen where each look from the animal weighs
more than a thousand lines of dialogue.

> Balthazar sees everything and judges nothing.

## The passion of an animal

Without a single word, the donkey becomes witness and victim, a mirror of a humanity
that has forgotten compassion. His journey is, quite literally, a way of the cross.

## Death among sheep

The final shot — Balthazar dying in a meadow, surrounded by a flock — is one of the most
serene and devastating images in cinema. No redemption is explained: only a creature
returning, without complaint, to the earth.
