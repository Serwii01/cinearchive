Cléo, a singer, awaits the result of a biopsy. Varda follows her for nearly two
continuous hours as the woman moves from fear of herself to the discovery of others.

## Object and subject

At first Cléo is an image: she checks every mirror, she lives to be looked at. Illness
forces her to reverse the gaze. When she stops looking at herself, she begins to see
Paris, people, light. Varda films that conversion with a freedom only the New Wave made
possible.

> As long as they look at me, I don't grow old.

## Time as protagonist

Title cards count the minutes: cinema synchronises with anguish. That structure is no
formal whim but pure empathy. We share her wait second by second.

## The stranger in the park

The closing encounter with a soldier resolves nothing and changes everything. Cléo stops
fearing death just as she begins, at last, to live among others.
