*Mulholland Drive* is not understood: it is recognised. Lynch starts from a failed
television pilot and transforms it into a nightmare about ambition, where the logic of
dreams replaces the logic of plot.

## Two women, two films

The first half is Betty's golden dream, newly arrived in Los Angeles with her smile
intact. The second is its bitter reverse: Diane, defeated, guilty. Lynch offers no
solution, only a hinge — a blue box — through which desire drains away.

> No hay banda. There is no band. It is all a recording.

## Club Silencio

In the key scene a singer performs *Crying* and collapses, yet the voice keeps sounding.
It is Lynch's thesis as a musical number: the emotion is real even when we know it's
fake. Like cinema. Like Hollywood. Like the love we tell ourselves in order not to wake.
