The film opens with its narrator dead, face down in a swimming pool. From there, Wilder
recounts how a struggling screenwriter ended up in the mansion of Norma Desmond, a
silent-film star left behind by sound and refusing to accept it.

## The ghost of an empire

Norma lives surrounded by her own past glory, watching her old films on a loop. Gloria
Swanson — herself a silent star — plays something close to herself, and that
biographical truth makes the role unbearably real.

> I am big. It's the pictures that got small.

## The industry as vampire

Wilder spares no one: not the star devoured by her myth, not the writer who sells out,
not a Hollywood that manufactures dreams and discards those who once embodied them.

## The final close-up

When Norma descends the staircase believing she is shooting again, looking into a camera
that is the camera of her madness, cinema gazes into the mirror and sees a made-up skull.
