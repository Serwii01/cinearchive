A wedding, a birth, a funeral. Between those extremes, *Yi Yi* unfolds the life of a
middle-class family in Taipei with a serenity that takes in, with no apparent effort,
everything it means to be alive.

## The observing camera

Yang often films through glass: windows, reflections, the city superimposed on faces. No
one is entirely alone, no one entirely accompanied. Modernity is that pane of glass which
joins and separates at once.

> Aren't we living half our lives without ever knowing it?

## The boy and the camera

Yang-Yang, the youngest son, photographs the backs of people's heads: he wants to show
them what they cannot see of themselves. In that innocent gesture lies the whole thesis of
Yang's cinema — to show us our own other side.

## The closing elegy

Before his grandmother's coffin, the boy says he feels old. *Yi Yi* ends where wisdom
begins: understanding that every life contains, in miniature, all the others.
