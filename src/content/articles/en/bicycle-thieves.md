A man finally lands a job: pasting up posters. For it he needs a bicycle. It is stolen
on his first day. On that elemental premise, De Sica raises the monument of Italian
neorealism.

## The street as studio

No sets, no stars: De Sica films a real labourer and his son crossing a Rome that still
smells of rubble. The city is not a backdrop but an antagonist — indifferent, infinite,
full of bicycles that may or may not be his.

> You live and you suffer. To hell with it.

## Father and son

What begins as a search ends as a fall. The gaze of the boy, Bruno, is the film's true
tribunal: he watches his father try to steal and understands, in an instant, that
poverty spares not even the good.

## No redemption

De Sica denies us comfort. There is no justice, no recovered bicycle. Only two hands
reaching for each other in the crowd. And that, incredibly, is enough.
