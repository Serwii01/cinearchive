*Stalker* isn't watched: it is traversed. Tarkovsky films a forbidden Zone where a
room grants one's deepest wishes, and turns the journey toward it into one of
cinema's most demanding and beautiful experiences.

## The Zone

Three men — the Guide, the Writer and the Scientist — make their way through an
abandoned industrial landscape that seems to breathe. The sepia of ordinary life
gives way to the damp green of the Zone, and that shift in colour is more narrative
than any explanation could be.

> Let what has to happen, happen. Let them believe. And let them laugh at their
> passions.

## Desire and fear

The film's true terror isn't what lies inside the room, but what the room reveals
about whoever enters. No character dares cross the threshold: they fear discovering
their most authentic desire, suspecting it won't match the one they confess.

## Material time

Tarkovsky sculpts time. The long takes aren't formal indulgence: they force us to
inhabit duration, to be bored even, until boredom dissolves into contemplation.
*Stalker* is cinema for those willing to lose themselves.
