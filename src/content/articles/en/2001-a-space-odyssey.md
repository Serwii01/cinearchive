There is a cut in *2001* worth the whole history of cinema: a bone hurled into the air
by a hominid becomes, in a single shot, a spacecraft. Four million years of evolution
resolved in one ellipsis. Kubrick does not explain — he trusts.

## The silence of space

Against science fiction's usual din, Kubrick films the void with its true sound: none.
Breath inside a helmet, a Strauss waltz turning like an orbital station. Technology
isn't spectacle here, it is choreography.

> I'm sorry, Dave. I'm afraid I can't do that.

## HAL, or the fear of our own making

The only human emotion in the film is felt by a machine. HAL lies, fears and dies
singing a lullaby. Kubrick inverts the terms: man has become functional; the
intelligence he created, tragic.

## The star child

The ending renounces all explanation and surrenders to the pure image. *2001* ends
where thought begins: before something we don't understand and which, even so,
recognises us.
